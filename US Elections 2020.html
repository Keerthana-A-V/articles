In United States, the president and the vice president is elected by the process of indirect election. The indirect election is done by  the citizens of the country, who are registered to vote in one of the U.S. fifty states or in Washington, DC, cast their votes for the members of ElectoralCollege under the  process of election.  The selected electoral then cast electoral votes to elect a president or vice president, the candidate is elected for their office by receiving majority votes. i. e  atleast 270 out of 538. The establishment of the Electoral College and  its procedure in  the U.S. Constitution  is under Article II, Section 1, Clauses 2 and 4. In case, no candidate receives an absolute majority of the votes for president, then the House of Representatives chooses the  most qualifying candidate as the president, and in case if no one receives the absolute majority of the votes for vice president , then the Senate has the power to elect the vice president. The election process was not specified in the constitution but was developed by the community. Any individual of eighteen years or above irrespective of their race, sex, color is eligible for voting.
